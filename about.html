what is pomodoro method?

the pomodoro method is a time management method developed by francesco cirillo in the late 1980s. it's a popular approach for enhancing productivity, and it is great for dealing with procrastination, time management, and distractions!

who is it good for?

procrastinators: if you tend to put off tasks or get overwhelmed easily, the pomodoro technique works great for breaking work into more manageable chunks!

students: studying often involves open-ended work that could take unlimited amounts of time, like studying for exams or working on assignments. the pomodoro method helps to encourage efficient study sessions and prevents burnout!

the easily distracted:short focus sessions followed by short breaks works great for handling interuptions! the pomodoro method helps to provide structure, accountability and allows for moments of distraction!

how does it work?

step 1: choose a task to work on

step 2: focus on the task for 25 minutes

step 3: take a five minute break

step 4: repeat steps 1-3 four times

step 5: take a long 15 or 30 minute break
